Dentures


 What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a titanium post (like a tooth root) that is surgically positioned into the jawbone beneath the gum line that allows your dentist to mount replacement teeth or bridge into that area. An implant doesn't come loose like a denture can. Dental implants also benefit general oral health because they do not have to be anchored to other teeth, like bridges.

 Why do we need Dental Implants?

If you are looking to replace missing or damaged teeth, dental implants are an excellent option. They are the most natural looking restoration option, and unlike dentures, you don't remove them for cleanings, and you can still eat the foods you love. You brush dental implants as you would your natural teeth, chew food with them, and to the untrained eye, they appear to be the teeth you were born with. That's why dental implants are the best option in dentistry outside of your natural teeth.

 What are the benifits of Dental Implants?

One of the biggest advantages of an implant is that it restores full chewing power. Most patients can’t tell the difference between their natural teeth and the implant tooth. They can eat with it completely normally, and they can brush and floss normally as well.

Whereas a dental bridge may only last around 10 years or so, dental implants can last a lifetime. The implant is made from titanium and integrates with the jawbone. It’s bio-compatible, meaning that it’s non-toxic and not rejected by the body. All in all it makes a powerful replacement tooth.

 Why choose Dental Implants over Dentures?

Dentures can slip out while speaking or eating (that's embarrasing), but Dental Implants are securely anchored and wont slip out.

Dentures often have to be kept in the mouth by using a dental adhesive. This sometimes requires re-glues after meals. A Dental Implant requires no adhesive.

Dentures have to be removed for cleaning, but a Dental Implant can be cleaned by normal brushing and flossing.

Mostly dentures also produce a clicking sound while eating, while a Denatal Implant doesn't produce any annoying sounds.
